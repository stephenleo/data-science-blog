üìö Multi-Document Keyphrase Extraction¬∂

Multi-Document Keyphrase Extraction (MDKE) is the task of extracting important phrases from a collection of documents.

  • MDKE can help analyze text clustering results to identify the important terms within each cluster of documents.

  • Contrary to single-document keyphrase extraction (SDKE), which has several well-known methods like RAKE, Text Rank, MultipartiteRank, and KeyBERT, MDKE is relatively under-studied in the literature.

  • The image below shows an example of SDKE in action.

In the paper ‚ÄúMulti-Document Keyphrase Extraction: A Literature Review‚Äù, the authors take two single-to-multi-document approaches as described below.

  • ‚úÖ Concat method: All the documents within a cluster are concatenated into a single big document and fed into the keyphrase extraction algorithm.

  • ‚úÖ Merge method: For each cluster, the keyphrase extraction algorithm is fed one document at a time, and keyphrases from all the individual documents are merged and ranked in descending order of their document frequency (the number of documents containing the keyphrase)

  • ‚úÖ There is some cleanup like stemming and de-duplication.
